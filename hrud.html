House Rental UX Design

OVERVIEW

R-WAREHOUSE is a housing website that offers affordable pricing options. The typical user’s are easy career professionals. R-Warehouse is to make acquiring, renting and buying of property easy, fast for all type of users.

ROLE

Product Designer, User Research, Interaction, Visual Design Prototyping and Testing

GOAL

The goal of this particular project was to design a R-WAREHOUSE Website to be user friendly by providing clear navigation and offering a fast checkout process.

Duration

October 2021 – December 2021

RESEARCH STUDY DETAILS

I conducted user interviews, which I then turned into empathy map to better understand the target user and their needs. I discovered that many target users treat online searching for properties to buy or rent as fun and relaxing activity. However, many online website are overwhelming and confusing to navigate, which frustrated many targeted users. This cause a normally enjoyable experience to become challenging for them, thereby defeating the purpose of fun derived from the activity.

PROCESS

The process at House Rental Website is based on the Normal Theory process. We aim to incorporate the key phases of Discovery, Definition, Ideation and Implementation in all of our projects.


GATHERING INSIGHTS

After collecting the recordings from the user interviews, I conducted affinity mapping with my teammate to synthesize the pains identified. We grouped these problems under common themes and features in the platform.


KEY CHALLENGES AND CONSTRAINTS

Navigation
Buying, Renting Property website designs are often busy, which results in confusing navigation

Interaction
Small button on the website makes selection of items dificult, which sometimes leads users to make mistakes.

Experience
Online Renting or buying website did not provide an engaging browsing experience

Layout
Layout and font size of text and other component are small and difficult to see thereby stressing the eyes to view them

Next , I sketch out paper wireframe s for each screen in my app, keeping the users pain points about navigation, browsing and checkout flow in mind. The home screen paper wireframe focuses on optimizing the browsing experience for users.

TAKEAWAYS

IMPACT:
Our target users shared that the design was intuitive to navigate through more engaging with the images, and demonstrated a clear visual hierarchy.

WHAT I LEARNED:
I learned that even a small design change can have a huge impact on a user experience. The most important takeaway for me is to always focus on the real need of the user when coming up with design ideas and solutions.
